A) For Paddy
## Water management for methane reduction (15)

1. Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD)
2. AWD using field water-tube monitoring (measured thresholds)
3. AWD with fewer flooding days (reduced continuous inundation)
4. Mid-season drainage (MSD)
5. Early-season drainage (shorten initial flooding period)
6. Pre-harvest drainage (timely drainage before harvest)
7. Intermittent irrigation scheduling (fixed wet–dry cycles)
8. Improved drainage outlets (faster drawdown)
9. Bund repair + leveling to enable controlled AWD (enables water control)
10. Laser land leveling (supports uniform AWD / less waterlogging)
11. Raised beds / furrow-irrigated rice (where applicable)
12. Aerobic rice management (non-flooded rice system)
13. Conversion of long-duration flooded rice to shorter-duration rice (less flooding time)
14. Use of short-duration varieties to reduce flooded period
15. Shift from transplanted puddled rice to systems reducing puddling/flooding duration (where defined)

---

## Crop establishment & field operations that reduce methane indirectly (8)

16. Direct-Seeded Rice (DSR) (reduces flooded nursery/puddling period)
17. Dry seeding (non-puddled establishment)
18. Reduced puddling intensity (where permitted/defined)
19. Strip till / reduced till rice establishment (where feasible)
20. Controlled traffic / minimized soil disturbance (site-dependent)
21. Improved field channels for precise irrigation (enables AWD)
22. Timely weeding to maintain aerobic periods (supports AWD feasibility)
23. Mechanized operations reducing repeated puddling passes (site-dependent)

*(Some of these are “enablers” — they matter because they make AWD/aerobic feasible and consistent for MRV.)*

---

## Nitrogen management for N₂O reduction (15)

24. Optimized N rate (reduce excess N)
25. Split N application (better timing reduces losses)
26. Deep placement of urea (UDP)
27. Controlled-release urea (CRU)
28. Nitrification inhibitors (where allowed)
29. Urease inhibitors (where allowed)
30. Site-Specific Nutrient Management (SSNM)
31. Leaf Color Chart (LCC)-guided N dosing
32. Basal N reduction with more top-dressing precision
33. Balanced fertilization (adequate P/K to reduce excess N needs)
34. Avoid N application just before heavy irrigation/rain (timing rule)
35. Replace part of urea with organic sources under planned rates (avoid over-application)
36. Precision fertilizer application (band placement, calibrated applicators)
37. Improved manure management timing (avoid anaerobic + N surplus conditions)
38. Inclusion of legumes in rotation where feasible (reduces synthetic N demand)

---

## Residue management and avoided burning (7)

39. No open-field burning of rice straw (avoided emissions)
40. Straw removal + off-field productive use (with leakage accounted)
41. Straw composting (instead of burning)
42. Straw incorporation with drainage/timing to reduce methane spikes (managed incorporation)
43. Straw mulching under non-flooded/managed water (method-dependent)
44. Use of straw for bioenergy with accounting (project/method dependent)
45. Collection/baling systems to prevent burning (enabler for #39)

---

## Soil carbon / durable carbon additions (5)

46. Biochar application (rice husk/straw biochar)
47. Compost application (stabilized organic matter)
48. Farmyard manure application under monitored rates (SOC improvement; method-dependent)
49. Green manuring (e.g., Sesbania/dhaincha) with proper water management (SOC + N dynamics)
50. Cover crop/legume in rice fallow (where feasible) to build SOC (rotation-based crediting)
